About Us

A society that unites on its strengths, can overcome its adversities. With your support we work to build integrated, people-centred responses to overcome adversities and inequities within communities.

Our goal is to create agency amongst people and to support programs that allow them to amplify their voices against challenges they face, to identify solutions and to initiate change.

Our vision is to become a catalyst for change through advocacy, awareness and capable multisectoral relationships.

Our Work

We focus on women, children, the elderly, migrants and rural communities who struggle to access basic services, where such services are central to better life and health outcomes.

We concentrate on specific areas within two provinces in South Africa and where the opportunity arises, we seek to create networks with international communities facing similar challenges.

Our belief is that narrative exchanges and story telling form an essential part of creating solutions for people, by themselves. We hope to reduce the reliance of vulnerable communities on the state, through increasing their self-reliance and innovation.

Our Organization

USAWA is an organisation birthed from Tekano, a fellowship for health equity and social justice. The directors are Global Atlantic Fellows for Health Equity at the Atlantic Institute, Oxford, United Kingdom.

Our Organisation prides itself for its facilitation and development capabilities to empower marginalized communities. Since inception we have worked to amplify the voices of children, supported communities impacted by civil unrest, responded to at-risk communities for flooding and continue to work on national and provincial programs for sustainable eye health interventions.

Partnerships

Our strength rests in collaboration.

We look forward to joining hands with people across all sectors and spheres of governance, and across the private and civil society sectors to build sustainable solutions towards a healthier and more equitable world.
